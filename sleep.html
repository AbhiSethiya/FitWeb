💡 Sleep & Recovery Tips

🕐
Consistent Sleep Schedule
Go to bed and wake at the same time daily — even on weekends. This anchors your circadian rhythm for deeper, more restorative sleep.
📵
Screen-Free Hour
Avoid screens 60 minutes before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin production and delays sleep onset by up to 2 hours.
🌡️
Cool Your Room
Keep your bedroom at 65–68°F (18–20°C). A cooler environment triggers the body's natural drop in core temperature needed for sleep.
☕
Caffeine Cutoff at 2 PM
Caffeine has a ~5-hour half-life. An afternoon coffee can still be active in your system at midnight, fragmenting sleep quality.
💧
Stay Well Hydrated
Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily. Dehydration impairs muscle recovery, cognitive function, and next-day performance.
🏃
Time Your Exercise
Regular exercise dramatically improves sleep quality. Avoid intense workouts within 2–3 hours of bedtime as they raise cortisol.
🧘
Build a Wind-Down Ritual
A consistent pre-sleep routine — reading, gentle stretching, or meditation — signals your brain it's time to transition to sleep.
🛏️
Bed is for Sleep Only
Avoid working or watching TV in bed. Your brain associates the bed with wakefulness if you use it for other activities.
